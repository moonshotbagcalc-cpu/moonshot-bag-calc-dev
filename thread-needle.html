What’s the difference between bag-making industrial machines?
Drop / Needle Feed
A bottom feed dog moves the material. On needle-feed models, the needle also moves with the material. These machines are generally used for flatter assemblies, linings, and lighter stacks.
Walking Foot
An upper walking foot and the lower feed dog move the layers while the needle remains stationary. This adds control through stacked canvas, denim, vinyl, leather, interfacing, zipper tape, and raised seam intersections.
Compound Walking Foot
The needle, inner presser foot, and feed dog move together while the outer foot alternates. This provides the most consistent feeding through thick layered assemblies and changes in seam thickness.
Point type controls how the needle enters the material. Choose the point for the material, then choose size for thread clearance and blade stiffness.
Coating is the bonus layer: it can reduce heat, sticking, residue, and wear. It does not replace the correct needle system, point, or size.
Needle systems are not universal. Always verify against your machine manual.
Thread and needle charts are starting points. Final results depend on your machine, bobbin case, tension range, feed system, presser foot pressure, material stack, and stitch length. Always test on a real sample before sewing your final bag.
